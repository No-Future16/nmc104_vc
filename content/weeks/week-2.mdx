In Week 2, we introduce the atomic building blocks of all visual media: line, shape, form, color, texture, space, and value. More importantly, we look at **how** human brains process these elements using Gestalt principles.

## The Gestalt Principles
Gestalt psychology argues that humans perceive entire patterns or configurations, not merely individual components.

1. **Proximity:** Items close together are perceived as a group.
2. **Similarity:** Items that look similar are perceived as related.
3. **Closure:** Our minds fill in missing information to create a complete shape.
4. **Continuity:** We prefer continuous paths and lines.
5. **Figure-Ground:** We separate focal objects (figure) from the background (ground).

<Alert type="warning">
**Design Tip:** If your design feels disorganized, check your proximity first. Group related items closer together and add whitespace between unrelated groups!
</Alert>
