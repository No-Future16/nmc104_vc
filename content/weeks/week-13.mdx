## The Purpose of a Portfolio
Your portfolio is not just an art gallery; it is a demonstration of your problem-solving abilities. Employers do not just want to see the final polished screen; they want to see *how you got there*. 

Ultimately, your **Final Project** for this course will be to build a personal portfolio website that showcases the work you've done throughout the semester.

### Crafting a Case Study
A strong UX/UI case study follows a clear narrative arc:
1. **The Problem:** What was the issue or brief? Who were the users?
2. **The Process:** Show your wireframes, your messy sketches, and your research.
3. **The Solution:** The final high-fidelity screens.
4. **The Reflection:** What went wrong? What did you learn?

## Personal Branding
You are a designer, so your personal brand should reflect intent.
- **Logo/Wordmark:** Keep it simple and legible.
- **Typography:** Choose 1-2 fonts that represent your personality (e.g., strict and brutalist, or soft and accessible).
- **Color Palette:** Pick a dominant color that makes you recognizable.

**Action Item:** Begin compiling the assets for your Personal Portfolio Website. You will need to export your Gestalt project, your Midterm social media campaign, and your Figma prototype.
