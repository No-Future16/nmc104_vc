## The Anatomy of Type
Understanding typography starts with understanding how typefaces are constructed. The two main categories you will work with are **Serif** (fonts with "feet" or decorative strokes at the ends of letters, like Times New Roman) and **Sans-Serif** (clean fonts without those strokes, like Helvetica or Arial).

### Key Typographic Terms
- **Leading (Line-height):** The vertical space between lines of text. Too tight, and the text is unreadable. Too loose, and it loses continuity.
- **Tracking (Letter-spacing):** The uniform spacing between all letters in a word. 
- **Kerning:** The spacing between two specific individual characters (usually adjusted by the typographer, but important for logos).

## Building Typographic Hierarchy
Just like visual elements, text needs hierarchy so users know what to read first, second, and third. You achieve this through:
1. **Size Category:** `H1` (Main Title) > `H2` (Subtitle) > `p` (Body text)
2. **Weight Contrast:** Bold titles paired with regular body text.

<Alert type="warning">
**Design Tip:** When pairing fonts, contrast is key. Do not pair two fonts that are too similar, like two different sans-serifs. Instead, pair a bold Sans-Serif title (like Space Grotesk) with a clean Serif body text (like Merriweather).
</Alert>
