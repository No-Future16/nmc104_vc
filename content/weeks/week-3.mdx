While design *elements* are the building blocks, design *principles* are the rules for how to put them together. This week focuses heavily on **Hierarchy** and **Color Theory**.

## Visual Hierarchy
Visual hierarchy is the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. This order is created by the visual contrast between forms in a field of perception. You can create hierarchy using:
- **Size:** Larger elements draw more attention.
- **Color:** Bright colors pop out against muted backgrounds.
- **Contrast:** High contrast elements are noticed first.
- **Alignment & Whitespace:** Elements set apart by whitespace demand attention.

## Color Psychology
Colors evoke emotions. In digital interfaces, color is also functional (e.g., Red for errors, Green for success, Blue for links).
