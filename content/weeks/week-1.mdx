## What is Digital Design?
Digital design is the practice of creating visual content for digital interfaces. Unlike print design, where the medium is fixed (a poster, a book), digital design lives on screens â€” meaning it has to adapt, move, and interact with the user.

### Key Differences from Print
- **Resolution vs. Dimensions:** Screens use pixels (72 ppi usually), while print uses physical measurements and high DPI.
- **Interactivity:** Digital designs have hover states, click events, and scroll behaviors.
- **Accessibility:** Digital design must account for screen readers, high contrast needs, and color blindness dynamically.

### Professional Digital Workflow
As a digital designer, your workflow will often mirror this course's structure:
1. **Theory:** Understanding the problem and the rules of design.
2. **Tools:** Selecting the right medium (Canva, Figma, Code).
3. **Execution (Project):** Building and iterating.
